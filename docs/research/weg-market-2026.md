# German WEG Market Research 2026

Last updated: 2026-06-06

This document captures the product reality behind RealEstate OS. A WEG
application wins only if it helps owners understand money, decisions, documents,
responsibilities, and deadlines better than their current manual process.

## Executive Summary

Small and mid-sized German owner communities face rising management costs,
limited availability of professional administrators, and increasing pressure to
keep legally relevant work traceable. The core obligations remain demanding:
annual budget, annual statement, asset report, owner meeting, minutes, decision
register, house money, reserves, maintenance, and transparent documentation.

The product should therefore behave less like a generic property dashboard and
more like a guided operating workspace. It must help non-experts act safely
without pretending to replace legal, tax, or construction advice.

## What Customers Want

| Customer need | Product implication |
| --- | --- |
| Confidence without legal training | Guided workflows, required fields, deadlines, explainers, and review states instead of blank forms. |
| Transparent money | Every amount needs a path to booking, receipt, category, distribution key, unit, and owner share. |
| Fast orientation | After login, an owner should immediately know what is open, what costs money, and what needs a decision. |
| Decisions that get implemented | A decision must create responsibility, tasks, due dates, documents, and implementation status. |
| Reliable communication | Requests need state, owner, history, due dates, and responsible people, not just free-form chat. |
| Self-management without chaos | Small WEGs need roles, checklists, templates, and clear separation between owners, board, and managers. |
| Planned maintenance | Reserves, measures, offers, decisions, special assessments, and implementation must be connected. |
| Low-friction participation | Less digital owners must be able to read, answer, approve, and find documents with minimal friction. |

## Recurring Pain Points

1. **Administrator scarcity and rising cost**
   Public industry reporting indicates price increases, capacity limits, and
   unprofitable mandates becoming a problem for smaller WEGs. This makes
   self-management and assisted management a real market, not a niche.

2. **Opaque finances**
   Annual statements, budgets, reserves, house money, arrears, and distribution
   keys are hard for owners to verify. Real value comes from explainability, not
   from another list of amounts.

3. **Fragile meetings and decisions**
   WEG work depends on invitation, agenda, voting, minutes, decision register,
   and follow-up. The product must support in-person, hybrid, and virtual
   contexts while keeping evidence attached.

4. **Decisions that fade after the meeting**
   Owners care whether work happens: get offers, award contract, track cost,
   upload evidence, inform owners, close the task.

5. **Scattered documents**
   Invoices, insurance policies, maintenance contracts, division declarations,
   minutes, and decisions need central storage and product context.

6. **Communication that creates distrust**
   Typical friction comes from missing answers, changing contacts, duplicate
   questions, and no shared history. Communication must become a case-like
   workflow.

7. **Self-management needs guardrails**
   Self-management depends on community size, conflict level, expertise, time,
   and maintenance risk. The product should expose risk and suggest external
   help when needed.

## Product Principles

1. **Guided over generic**
   Every core job needs required context, validation, next action, and review
   state.

2. **Everything is explainable**
   Finance, decisions, tasks, documents, and communication must connect through
   evidence and audit.

3. **Owners understand their share**
   Community views must lead to owner views: what affects me, what do I owe,
   what do I vote on, what evidence do I need?

4. **Workflow before storage**
   A document is not just a file. A message is not just text. A decision is not
   just a protocol line. Each belongs to a product case.

5. **Trust through calm design**
   The interface should show control: clear language, priorities, status,
   deadlines, numbers, and responsibility.

6. **Self-management with guardrails**
   The product should identify risk cases and route users toward external
   advice or formal decisions when the workflow is beyond simple tooling.
